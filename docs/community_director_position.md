=== About OPeNDAP ===

OPeNDAP is both the name of a non-profit organization and the name of
a protocol that the OPeNDAP organization has developed. OPeNDAP
provides software that allows people to access and subset local data
from remote locations regardless of local storage format. Founded in
2000, OPeNDAP has a small 5-person, remote, and full-time team, and a
supportive 5-person Board of Directors. Currently, its main funder is
NASA. 

=== About the position ===

OPeNDAP seeks to hire a full-time Community Director to help develop
our community engagement approach, and grow and foster our community
of users, contributors, developers, and institutional partners. This
is a new upper-level role in the organization offering an opportunity
to strategically build our community. Our goals for this hire are to
create new pathways for people to contribute to our software
development, make it easier for people to understand and use OPeNDAP
software and protocols, and develop new collaborations for OPeNDAP. 

The best candidate would be a self-starter, resourceful, friendly, a
connector of people, and passionate about open source software
development. As part of the role, the candidate is encouraged to
engage in their own professional development and research activities. 

=== Key responsibilities of the role: ===

Strategically develop the community development approach for OPeNDAP, including providing leadership and vision for how best to develop the community. 
Hands-on management of the community, including developing and supporting small working groups, leading and implementing communication with community members and the community at-large, and planning and implementing community events. 
Stewardship of community leaders or champions.
Coordinate and manage small project teams.
Develop institutional-level and project-level partnerships and collaborations. 
Publically represent OPeNDAP as a thought leader and subject matter expert. 
Evaluate and assess the community approach. 
Contribute to executive-level strategic development of the organization itself. 
A passion for community engagement and enthusiasm to build connections between people and cultivate new community leaders.
